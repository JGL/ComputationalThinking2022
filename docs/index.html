Lecture and Workshop slides for the Introduction to Creative Computing 2022 Intensive by Joel Gethin Lewis

Schedule:

Monday 4th April 2022 - Friday 8th April 2022, online.

Daily schedule (all times are London local time):

  • Morning session:
    • 1000 - 1100: Lecture
    • 1100 - 1230: Workshop
    • 1230 - 1300: Discussion
  • Lunch: 1300 - 1400
  • Afternoon session:
    • 1400 - 1500: Lecture
    • 1500 - 1630: Workshop
    • 1630 - 1700: Discussion

Structure:

  • Monday 4th April 2022:
    • Morning session, Introduction:
      • Lecture 1: Introduction.
      • Workshop 1: signing up to GitHub, learning Git, using editor.p5js.org, downloading VSCode, using VSCode with p5.js on your own computer, running a local server, duplicating a p5.js example on editor.p5js.org and locally.
      • Discussion 1: Introductions and Wu-Tang names and connections.
    • Afternoon session, Counting and Remembering:
      • Lecture 2: Counting and Remembering.
      • Workshop 2: Binary <-> Decimal. CamelCase. Converting between types. Data Self Portraits.
      • Discussion 2: What's in a name?
  • Tuesday 5th April 2022:
    • Morning session, Functions and Drawing:
      • Lecture 3: Functions and Drawing.
      • Workshop 3: Drawing Your Initials and Draw, then Code.
      • Discussion 3: Lilian Schwartz.
    • Afternoon session, Deciding and Looping:
      • Lecture 4: Deciding and Looping.
      • Workshop 4: Left or Right, Billiard Ball, Seven Circles and Transitioning Rectangles.
      • Discussion 4: Sol Lewitt, Yayoi Kusama and The Tetralemma.
  • Wednesday 6th April 2022:
    • Morning session, Arrays and Classes:
      • Lecture 5: Arrays and Classes.
      • Workshop 5: Colour Observation, Living Line I and Ripples.
      • Discussion 5: Creative uses of multiple dimensions and Timothy Morton.
    • Afternoon session, Randomness and Noise:
      • Lecture 6: Randomness and Noise.
      • Workshop 6: Coin toss, Drunk Walk I, II and III.
      • Discussion 6: Vera Molnar and Alan Moore.
  • Thursday 7th April 2022:
    • Morning session, Scaling and Waves:
      • Lecture 7: Scaling and Waves.
      • Workshop 7: lerp() and map(), Butt Generator, Circle using Sine and Cosine.
      • Discussion 7: Anni Albers and Bridget Riley.
    • Afternoon session, Time and Algorithms:
      • Lecture 8: Time and Algorithms.
      • Workshop 8: Collage Machine, Colour of a Pixel, Searching for the Brightest Point.
      • Discussion 8: Tehching Hsieh, the History of Computing, Every Icon, RadicalArt.info.
  • Friday 8th April 2022:
    • Morning session, Feedback and Emergence:
      • Lecture 9: Feedback and Emergence.
      • Workshop 9: Flocking and Conway's Game of Life.
      • Discussion 9: Joscha Bach and a Computational Universe.
    • Afternoon session, State Machines and Libraries:
      • Lecture 10: State Machines and Libraries.
      • Workshop 10: p5.js Sound Library, External Libraries, Rita.js and Matter.js.
      • Discussion 10: Where next?.
